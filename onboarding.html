SCS Youth Code of Conduct

Sitka Conservation Society Youth Code of Conduct

The SCS Code of Conduct applies to all activities coordinated through SCS including local, district, state and national activities.

While attending SCS activities and events, I will:

  • Obey all rules established by the SCS program, the affiliate program partners, and all local, state and federal laws. Conduct myself in a courteous manner and be respectful of the authority of adult volunteers, youth leaders, SCS staff and others in leadership roles.
  • Not use, accept or carry alcohol, drugs or tobacco, or associate with or remain in the presence of others using the substances.
  • Know that the possession or use of firearms is prohibited, except when part of an approved shooting sports educational program.
  • Respect all persons, facilities and vehicles. I will be responsible for any damage caused resulting from my behavior.
  • Help others have a pleasant experience by making every attempt to include all participants in activities.
  • Be in the assigned program area (for example: dorms, cabins, programs, etc.) at all times.
  • Use appropriate language and dress appropriately for each event.
  • Acknowledge that searches of personal property may take place when there is reasonable suspicion of violations of law or policies.

While attending overnight events, I will also:

  • Not leave the activity or event unless permission is secured from the adult in charge.
  • Be in my sleeping area and honor established curfews.
  • Not enter the sleeping areas of members of the opposite gender and will I not invite non SCS participants to the sleeping areas.

I have read the SCS Code of Conduct and agree to live up to these expectations while participating in SCS programs and events. I am aware that my actions and decisions affect others. I understand that my failure to act with good character could result in consequences, including dismissal from the event or program. I am willing to accept the appropriate consequences of my actions.

This code of conduct has been gratefully adopted from SCS's professional partner in youth programming, University of Alaska Fairbanks Cooperative Extension's 4-H program.
